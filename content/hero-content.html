Theoretical Deep Learning • Carnegie Mellon University

Hi, I’m a third-year PhD candidate at Carnegie Mellon University. I am interested in the theoretical foundations of deep learning—formulating governing “laws” analogous to those in physics—and translating those insights into practical innovations. I am recently working on the mathematical underpinnings of large language model architectures and developing novel, theory-driven modules to enhance their expressivity and efficiency. I am also using information theory to analyze LLM capabilities and derive effective theories that explain and predict their behavior.
